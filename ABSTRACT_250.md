# The 250-word abstract (submission-form; seeds ICAIF/OSF/pitches; swaps into main.tex at the Q4 restructure)

Whether a non-stationary market rewards memory is measurable before any
retention machinery is bought. We introduce a one-number diagnostic —
Γ̂, the post-reactivation decision-regret gap between a capacity-bounded,
reward-driven learner and an oracle-pinned retainer — and validate it
the hard way: pre-registered at every step, with every refutation on
the public record. In controlled markets, retention and invariance form
a super-additive 2×2 (100 seeds; joint p = 6×10⁻²³), the emergent pool
matches a hand-pinned oracle it is never shown, and forgetting emerges
under a shared neural trunk with nothing evicted (p = 2×10⁻⁷⁸). On real
data, Γ̂'s sign has tracked the outcome direction of every resolved
test — dissociations where positive, inversions where negative — across
two eras of U.S. industry returns (8/12 strong-form, 12/12 under the
one-directional weak form;
all misses disclosed), surviving leave-one-event-out, era blocks, and
crisis-window transaction costs, which amplify rather than erase the
deficit. One cell is externally certified: an announcement-lagged
NBER-recession labeler with no free threshold, lodged with OSF before
the data join, resolved as a hit. The mechanism on real markets is
honestly open — the deficit behaves as a persistent allocation gap, not
demonstrated forgetting — and the discriminating tests are lodged in
advance: a measurement-split live prediction (frozen June 2026, scored
April 2027) and a T-split CRSP battery. All claims are regret claims,
not alpha claims; code, data, and a 349-claim machine audit are
released. (~245 words)
